Caregiver Eligibility

Who may qualify to become a paid caregiver?

Caregiver applicants generally need to meet program, documentation, and verification requirements before they can provide paid support through Michigan care programs.

General caregiver requirements may include:

  • Be at least 18 years of age
  • Be legally authorized to work in the United States
  • Have a valid Social Security Number or Tax Identification Number
  • Complete Michigan CHAMPS provider enrollment
  • Pass required background screening and program verification checks
  • Be selected and approved by the Home Help participant
View Full Eligibility Details

Caregivers should be ready to follow care plans.

Applicants should be physically and mentally able to provide care safely, follow program requirements, maintain timesheets, and communicate clearly with the person receiving care and the care team.

Caregiver Role

What caregivers may help with.

Care needs vary by person. A caregiver may support daily routines, safety, comfort, companionship, and care-related tasks based on the individual’s plan.

Personal Care Support

Assistance may include hygiene, grooming, dressing, mobility support, and other daily living needs.

Home-Based Assistance

Caregivers may help individuals stay comfortable, safe, and supported in their home environment.

Companionship

Friendly communication and emotional support can help reduce isolation and improve daily comfort.

Following Care Plans

Caregivers should follow assigned care expectations, program requirements, and documentation responsibilities.
