Family Members Who May Qualify

A trusted person chosen by the participant may be able to provide care.

Under program rules, certain family members and trusted individuals may be considered as caregivers when they meet requirements and are approved by the participant.

Adult Children

Adult children may be considered when they meet caregiver requirements and are selected by the participant.

Grandchildren & Siblings

Grandchildren, brothers, and sisters may be eligible depending on the program situation and approval process.

Grandparents & Relatives

Grandparents and other relatives may qualify when they can safely provide care and meet documentation requirements.

Friends, Neighbors & Trusted People

A trusted friend, neighbor, or individual chosen by the participant may also be considered if program rules are met.

Important Program Note

Some caregiver relationships are generally not eligible under Home Help rules.

While many trusted individuals may qualify, certain relationships are usually restricted under the Michigan Home Help Program.

  • A spouse caring for their husband or wife is generally not eligible for payment through this program.
  • Parents caring for their own minor child are generally not eligible under the Home Help Program.

Questions & Answers

Common questions families ask about caregiver support.

These answers are designed to give general guidance. For your specific situation, contact our team so we can help you understand the next step.

Who can become a paid caregiver?

A paid caregiver may be a qualified family member, relative, friend, neighbor, or trusted person chosen by the participant, as long as program requirements and approval steps are completed.

Can a family member get paid to provide care?

In many cases, certain family members may be considered. Eligibility depends on the relationship, program rules, caregiver requirements, and approval by the participant.

What is CHAMPS enrollment?

CHAMPS is part of Michigan’s provider enrollment process. Caregivers may need to complete CHAMPS enrollment before they can be approved for certain program-related services.

Who chooses the caregiver?

The Home Help participant generally selects and approves the caregiver. The caregiver must still meet program requirements and complete necessary verification steps.

Can a spouse be paid as a caregiver?

A spouse caring for their husband or wife is generally not eligible for payment through the Home Help Program. Our team can help explain what options may apply.

What documents may be needed?

Documents may include identification, work authorization details, SSN or Tax ID information, enrollment forms, background screening items, and other supporting paperwork.

How can Michigan Family Caregiver LLC help?

We can help with eligibility screening, CHAMPS enrollment guidance, Home Help application steps, paperwork support, and ongoing caregiver program navigation.

How do I know if I qualify?

The best way is to contact our team. We can review your situation, explain common requirements, and guide you toward the right next step.
